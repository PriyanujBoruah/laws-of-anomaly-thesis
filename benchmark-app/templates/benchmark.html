Model & Metric Information

Regression Metrics

  • MAE (Mean Absolute Error): The average absolute difference between the predicted and actual values. Lower is better.
  • MSE (Mean Squared Error): The average of the squares of the errors. Penalizes larger errors more heavily. Lower is better.
  • RMSE (Root Mean Squared Error): The square root of MSE, putting the error back into the original units. Lower is better.
  • R2 (R-squared): Represents the proportion of the variance in the dependent variable that is predictable from the independent variable(s). Higher is better (closer to 1).
  • RMSLE (Root Mean Squared Log Error): RMSE on the log-transformed values. Useful when targets have a wide range. Lower is better.
  • MAPE (Mean Absolute Percentage Error): The average of the absolute percentage errors. Expresses error as a percentage. Lower is better.

Classification Metrics

  • Accuracy: The proportion of correct predictions among the total number of cases. Higher is better.
  • AUC (Area Under the Curve): Measures the ability of a classifier to distinguish between classes. Higher is better (closer to 1).
  • Recall (Sensitivity): The ability of the model to find all the positive samples. Higher is better.
  • Prec. (Precision): The ability of the model not to label as positive a sample that is negative. Higher is better.
  • F1-Score: The weighted average of Precision and Recall. A good balance between them. Higher is better.
  • Kappa: A statistic that measures inter-rater agreement for categorical items. Higher is better.
